    trending_down "Economic Engine" Myth

    The Pitch
    • ECONOMIC ENGINE
    • INCREDIBLE OPPORTUNITY
    • REVITALIZED INFRASTRUCTURE
    Promises of growth often ignore the displacement of existing spending.
    The Reality
    Local Businesses Lost expand_more

    The Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis identifies a "substitution effect" where consumers divert spending from local restaurants and theaters to the casino. If the clientele is local, this results in a net loss for existing businesses.

    Source open_in_new
    Bankruptcies expand_more

    Counties that legalize casino gambling experience a 2.3% higher annual growth rate in personal bankruptcies compared to non-casino counties. Bankruptcy rates eventually exceed those of non-casino counties after nine years of operations.

    Source open_in_new
    Poverty expand_more

    Despite casinos generating revenue, poverty rates for Native American communities with casinos remain higher than the national average (19.6% vs 12.1%). The "economic engine" argument often fails to account for the lack of wealth distribution to the poorest residents.

    Source open_in_new
    Foreclosure expand_more

    While foreclosure specific data varies, the sharp rise in personal bankruptcies and "bad debt" in casino counties directly correlates to housing instability and loss of assets.

    Source open_in_new
    Bad Debt expand_more

    Gambling debt is often hidden due to shame, but studies show significant financial harm to families. "Affected others" (family/friends) report severe stress, ill health, and relationship breakdown due to the gambler's debt.

    Source open_in_new

    work_off "Jobs" Promise

    The Pitch
    • GOOD PAYING JOBS
    • RETAIN TALENT
    "Good jobs" that pay less than fast food?
    The Reality
    The "5,520 Jobs" Myth expand_more

    The "5,520 Jobs" headline is a deception. They are mostly temporary construction jobs. Once the concrete dries, those workers leave, and the taxpayers are left with the 30-year social bill. In a tight labor market, the 1,024 projected permanent jobs gains will likely be negated by job losses from local small businesses (restaurants, bars, etc.) that are cannibalized by the casino.

    Low Wage Jobs expand_more

    The data specifically regarding Full House Resorts' 'Rising Star Casino' shows that essential service roles pay significantly less than the current market rate for fast food workers in Fort Wayne. Line Cooks at the casino earn $13.25-$17.75/hr vs $20.27/hr at local fast food chains.

    Source open_in_new
    Embezzlement expand_more

    A Dept. of Justice study found that arrestees identified as pathological gamblers were 3 to 5 times more likely to be arrested than the general population, often committing robbery, assault, or theft to finance their addiction.

    Source open_in_new

    • COMMUNITY WELL-BEING
    • HUMANITARIAN FUND
    • NEIGHBORHOOD IMPROVEMENTS

    The Reality
    Addiction expand_more

    Research indicates that 96% of individuals with gambling disorder have a co-occurring psychiatric disorder. The American Psychiatric Association defines it as a disorder leading to clinically significant impairment, where sufferers exhaust savings and damage family relationships.

    Source open_in_new
    Homelessness expand_more

    A University of Cambridge study found that homeless individuals are 10 times more likely to be problem gamblers than the non-homeless population, establishing a "bi-directional" link where gambling leads to homelessness and vice versa.

    Source open_in_new
    Mental Health Issues expand_more

    Gambling disorder has the highest suicide attempt rate of any addiction. In one state study, 23.2% of those meeting criteria for gambling disorder had attempted suicide.

    Source open_in_new
    Substance Abuse expand_more

    There is a high comorbidity between gambling and substance abuse. Pathological gamblers have a 5.5 times higher risk for substance use disorders compared to non-gamblers, often using drugs or alcohol to cope with losses.

    Source open_in_new
    Fatal Accidents expand_more

    A study in the Journal of Health Economics estimates that alcohol-related fatal traffic accidents increase by approximately 9.2% in counties that open casinos.

    Source open_in_new

    • WORLD-CLASS DESTINATION
    • PREMIER DESTINATION

    The Reality
    Human Trafficking expand_more

    The American Gaming Association admits that casinos are used by traffickers to "facilitate criminal activity" and "seek out potential buyers." Survivors report that traffickers use the anonymity of casino environments to mask the movement and exploitation of victims.

    Source open_in_new
    Corruption expand_more

    Empirical models indicate a "Granger causality" where casino revenues can "cause" political corruption. The large cash flows attract rent-seeking behavior from politicians and can lead to the erosion of public sector integrity.

    Source open_in_new
    Domestic Violence expand_more

    Women whose partners have a gambling problem are 10.5 times more likely to experience intimate partner violence. Nearly 25% of pathological gamblers report engaging in spousal abuse.

    Source open_in_new
    Child Neglect expand_more

    A systematic review found a significant association between problem gambling and multiple forms of child maltreatment, including physical abuse and neglect. The financial strain and time consumption of gambling directly erode parental capacity.

    Source open_in_new
    Divorces expand_more

    The National Gambling Impact Study Commission reports a lifetime divorce rate of 39.5% for problem gamblers, compared to 18.2% for the general population. 80% of divorced problem gamblers cite gambling as a significant factor in the split.

    NET GROWTH

    The primary economic argument for the Fort Wayne casino is that it will generate "new" money. This assumes it functions as a tourist destination. However, in markets lacking high-density attractions, casinos operate as "convenience gambling" venues, leading to the Substitution Effect.

    sync_problem Mechanics of the Substitution Effect

    For a casino to generate net growth, it must function as an "export" industry—bringing in outside dollars. If it merely recirculates local capital, the net impact is neutral or negative.

    Research by the Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis notes that in mid-sized urban areas, casinos often correlate with a stagnation or decrease in local retail sales. The casino acts as a gravitational well, absorbing liquidity that formerly supported small businesses.

    "243" Coefficient

    For every $1,000 increase in casino revenue, businesses within a 10-30 mile radius experience a loss of approximately $243 in sales.

    Legal Fact Check

    THE "TRIBAL THREAT" DECEPTION

    Mayor Tucker and Senator Busch claim a "Tribal Casino" is inevitable if their commercial one isn't approved. Federal law indicates this is a manufactured lie designed to panic State House and Senate voters and justify their agenda.

    warning

    "Fritz Road" Scare Tactic

    The administration argues that the Miami Tribe of Oklahoma will open a tax-free casino on their Fritz Road land if we don't act. A textualist analysis of the Indian Gaming Regulatory Act (IGRA) shows three formidable legal walls blocking this path.

    event_busy
    Wall #1

    1988 Cutoff

    IGRA prohibits gaming on land acquired after Oct 1988. The Fritz Road land was acquired in June 2024—nearly 36 years too late. It is presumptively ineligible.

    history
    Wall #2

    86-Year Gap

    Exceptions require a "temporal connection." The Miami Tribe was recognized in 1938. Acquiring land 86 years later fails this test. It is not an open-ended license.

    gavel
    Wall #3

    Governor's Veto

    The only remaining path requires the Governor's Concurrence. No Indiana Governor would approve a tax-free competitor to state casinos. It is a political dead end.

    zoom_in

    State Senator Justin Busch and Mayor Sharon Tucker pose for local news station coverage, creating a false sense of urgency to circumvent a voter referendum as teenagers play frisby on the Fritz Road property.

    gavel

    Verdict

    The Miami Tribe's land is for a Cultural Resources Extension Office (CREO), not a casino. The "Tribal Option" is not a legal reality—it is a rhetorical strategy designed to manufacture urgency.

    science Research Methodology

    travel_explore Distance-Based Risk Modeling

    Populations living closer to a casino experience significantly higher rates of gambling addiction. Our model applies distance-decay multipliers based on peer-reviewed research:

    • < 10 Miles: 2.0x Risk Multiplier
    • 10 - 20 Miles: 1.5x Risk Multiplier
    • > 20 Miles: 1.0x (Baseline State Average)

    calculate Problem Gambler Estimation

    New Addicts = Σ (Pop × Base_Rate × Multiplier)

    *Population data sourced from US Census Bureau (2020 Decennial) at Block Group level.

    *Baseline rate derived from National Council on Problem Gambling (NCPG) surveys.

    attach_money Social Cost Valuation

    We calculate the economic impact (bankruptcy, crime, divorce, job loss) per addict using inflation-adjusted values.
